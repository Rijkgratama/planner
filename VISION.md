# Lock In Planner — Vision

A living document for where this project is heading. We hold every decision up
against this: *does it serve the vision?*

## The one-line vision

A personal AI planning assistant that feels like a **calm coach in your corner** —
helping you fill each day with a *doable* amount while making sure your main goals
still get done, and gently keeping the other things that matter from falling
through the cracks.

## The feeling

- **Smooth and modern.** Opening the app should *lower* stress, not add to it.
  Looking at your planning should feel calm and in control.
- **A coach in your corner.** Supportive, on your side, helping you become the
  person you're trying to be.

## The core job

Fill each day with a **doable** amount of work, so days feel achievable — while
making sure the **main goals still get done**.

## Goals vs. essentials

- **Main goals come first.** Right now that's mostly *exams* — the things that
  genuinely have to be handled. The app should keep the focus there.
- **But the real value is balance.** What makes this app worth using is that it
  weaves in the *other* things that matter — exercise, the dog, chores — so they
  don't get crowded out by the big stuff. Finding that balance is the point.

## The coach personality (nudging)

- **Proactive but never annoying.** Good timing, light touch. Nudges help; nagging
  doesn't.
- **Gentle check-ins on loose recurring things**, e.g. *"hey, did you get the
  laundry done this week?"* for a "at least once a week" type task.

## Non-negotiables

- None locked in yet — to be discovered as we go. (Earlier candidates worth
  revisiting: stays private/local, always feels like yours to control.)

## Build direction (how we get there)

1. **Read-only assistant first** — the AI can *see* your tasks/routines and propose
   a plan or advice, but you approve before anything changes. Low risk.
2. **Then let it act** — give the AI tools to add/move/skip tasks and routines,
   with your confirmation. This is where it becomes a real assistant.

(See the conversation history / README for the current app architecture: a static,
client-only PWA storing everything in localStorage.)
